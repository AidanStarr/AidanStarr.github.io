Aidan Starr

What can I do?

  • In the office you can find me working with:
    • Chronostratigraphy: Constructing age-depth models for sediment cores by a variety of approaches, classical and novel
    • MATLAB: 8+ Years experience using Matlab for data analysis, statistics, visualisation
    • Python: The majority of my data analysis happens in Python (Jupyter, NumPy, Pandas, Xarray, Seaborn, Matplotlib, etc...)
    • R: I also do much of my time series analysis in R, with the help of packages such as Astrochron and GeochronR.
    • Ocean Data View for visualising oceanographic data
    • LaTeX: Typsetting documents in the tex language
    In general, I try to take a data science inspired approach to work. You can find much of my code and data on Github.

  • I like to make numbers into other numbers with:
    • Time Series Analysis: Analysing regularly- and irregularly-spaced data in the frequency domain (Blackman-Tukey, Lomb-Scargle, SSA, Wavelet Analysis, Multi-Taper Method, ARIMA modelling, etc.)
    • Novel numerical approaches to explore paleoclimate datasets
    • Age-uncertain statistics (using age ensemble age models, for example)
  • My primary lab-based activities include:
    • Washing and seiving of marine sediments for paleoclimate analysis
    • Sediment grain size analysis (Coulter Counter, Malvern MultiSizer X) - Currently in the process of setting up a "Sortable Silt" protocol at Rutgers Unviersity
    • Identification and picking of Neogene Foraminifera (planktic and benthic)
    • Stable isotope analysis of calcite (Thermo MAT 253 [Kiel IV])
    • Identification and counting of ice-rafted Debris, pyrite, volcanic ash, and other sand-sized entities in marine sediment (light microscope and SEM)
    I also have some experience with measuring bulk chemistry (XRF and ICP-OES) and less common isotope systems (e.g. He) in marine sediments.
